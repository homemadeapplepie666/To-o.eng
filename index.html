► PERSONAL BACKGROUND // HISTORIA
[ CHILDHOOD ]
Grew up in a crowded house with his parents and three siblings near the lake. Poor but happy. His mamá called him her "little monster" because he'd regrow scraped knees before she could finish bandaging them. Learned to swim before he could walk properly.
[ TEEN YEARS ]
At fifteen, he was out with the local troublemakers when the Hartfield Agricultural Corporation bulldozed his family home to break ground on a processing plant. His parents and siblings were inside. The company claimed ignorance. No investigation. No compensation. Toño watched the rubble from across the street, gum going stale in his mouth.
[ NOW ]
Lives alone, survives alone. Sells what he can regenerate—fingers, patches of skin, the occasional organ through very illegal channels—to whoever pays. Shoplifts when the money runs dry. The old folks in town feed him sometimes; they remember his mamá. He tells himself this is temporary. Has been telling himself that for seven years.

⚠ NOTA DEL AUTOR // AUTHOR'S NOTE

Toño's regeneration is complete and relatively fast—a severed finger regrows in about a week, internal organs take longer. The process is exhausting and leaves him ravenously hungry. He's sold parts of himself more times than he can count. It doesn't hurt less with repetition. He just got better at smiling through it.

Underneath the jokes and schemes is a young man running on spite, survival instinct, and the desperate hope that tomorrow might be different. He's not bad. He's just been left behind by everyone who should have protected him.
